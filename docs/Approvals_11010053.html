RTB Cat Help Wiki : Approvals

Created by Jen Brannstrom, last modified on Apr 28, 2017



Ad-approvals are done by the approvals team at AppNexus. They do this on behalf of all the exchanges. Google does a secondary check,

AppNexus does have an appeals-line, if you see that the approvals-policy has been incorrectly interpreted we can raise a ticket with them. Please do not abuse this privilige.

Ads may never:

  1. Be deceptive or offensive.
  2. Be provocative, sexually suggestive, and/or pornographic.
  3. Include any illegal or objectionable content.
  4. Have excessive animations. Infinitely looping creatives are fine but if the animations are so aggressive that they could be seen as extreme they are not allowed.
  5. Use celebrities or trademarked brands to imply endorsements when they have not expressly endorsed the advertisers' product.

Creative Review: All creatives will be automatically submitted for review by compliance when they are uploaded, and will remain in "pending" status until they have been reviewed. Creatives are reviewed on working days, 9 am to 5 pm EST - on average it will take less than 12 hours. 

It is very important that any creatives you upload do not make untrue claims, whether expressly or implied. If you have any doubt about a creative, please send it to us directly at support@rtb.cat and we will let you know if it is appropriate.

Sweepstakes Campaigns

Sweepstakes campaigns must make it clear to the user that they only have a "chance" at winning. If there is any ad copy hinting towards an automatic or guaranteed prize and it is not backed up, the campaign will be rejected.

Here are some examples of safe ad copy:

  • "Want to win? Click here for details"
  • "You could win a prize! Click here for more info"
  • "Want to win an iPhone? Learn more"
  • "Free iPhone! Details apply"
  • "There are limited prizes available"

Sweepstakes campaigns are commonly rejected for using:

  • Copyright infringement (facebook interface, twitter banners, "iPhone testers needed" etc)
  • Countdown timers, false urgency
  • False claims like:
    • "Only (3) prizes left"
    • "You are today's lucky visitor"
    • "You must complete this page within 2 minutes or your chance will be given to someone else"
    • "Get an instant prize"
    • "Your iPhone has been reserved - claim your iPhone"

Landing page approvals

Landing page URLS may not rotate. You can of course split-test pages, but they must be on the same domain. You can't switch between subdomains either.

Landing pages may never:

  1. Be deceptive or offensive.
  2. Be provocative, sexually suggestive, and/or pornographic.
  3. Lead to the placement of malware, spyware, viruses, etc.
  4. Lead to any kind of auto-download software, app, etc.
  5. Include any illegal/objectionable content.

Use celebrities/trademarked brands to imply endorsements when they have not expressly endorsed the advertisers' product.

Non-Targeted Traffic: This policy applies to all renderings of a campaign - including landing pages, offer pages, redirects, out-of-geo renderings, etc. All pages that your offer leads to must be compliant.

Landing Page Compliance: Campaigns will be automatically submitted to compliance when they are uploaded, and will remain in "pending" status until they have been reviewed. Campaigns are reviewed on working days, 9 am to 5 pm EST - on average they will be reviewed within 12 hours.

Ad Copy: Misleading vs Appropriate

This section aims to clarify what ad copy is too deceptive, and what appropriate ad copy it can be replaced with. Please note that this is general information, and campaign approvals will vary by exchange.

If the creative implies there is something wrong with the user's phone, it is likely to be rejected.

  • Example: creatives that use the ad copy "fix", "clean", "upgrade", or "optimize" are often rejected for implying the user's phone is damaged or outdated. Ad copy like "help", "increase", "improve" is better.
  • Example: creatives that state "Your phone is slow - accelerate now" implies that the user's phone needs to be fixed, which is too misleading. This could be replaced with something like "click here to accelerate your phone".

Rejected:

If the creative implies that an "update" or "upgrade" is required/recommended, it is likely to be rejected.

  • Example: LPs and creatives that use the ad copy "To update, install the app and open it" or "Your android can take advantage of the latest update" imply that the user has the app and is required to make a change, which is not necessarily true.
  • Example: LPs and creatives that use the ad copy "Upgrade recommended!" implies the user needs to perform an action to improve their phone. Instead, say "get this app to improveâ€¦"

Rejected:

If the creative or landing page heavily implies that the user has won something, it is likely to be rejected.

  • Example: ad copy that says "Free iPhone" or "Click to win" should be replaced with something like "You could win an iPhone" or "Play to win" or "Click here for a chance to win".
  • Example: ad copy that says "There are only (3) prizes left!" should be replaced with "limited quantities available".
  • Example: ad copy that says "you have been randomly selected - you are today's lucky user!" should be replated with "you have a chance at a prize!".

MoPub

MoPub is the biggest exchange and offers high quality traffic. As such, they have the strictest compliance requirements that must be followed.

Best Practice to get campaigns/creatives approved on MoPub:

  1. It is clear to the user what the offer is before they click on the ad
  2. State the app/offer name clearly
  3. State the app's function clearly
  4. Use the logo that matches the app/offer
  5. Use a call to action that is a word, relevant, and clear like "Download App" or "Play"
  6. The landing page must clearly state what is being subscribed to, the fees, etc
  7. The creatives, the landing page, and the offer must all be clearly matched

MoPub strictly prohibits any content that may mislead users, including:

  1. Windows Dialog or Alert Style banners (ie creatives that a user might mistake for an OS- or application-level notification rather than an advertisement, including creatives that mimic buttons or icons or claim a functionality that is not there)
  2. Ads that do not ask the user for permission before initiating any downloads/services/fees. This means Click-to-Call and Click-to-Subscribe ads must include an intermediary landing page that clearly explains the terms and/or fees associated with the call or subscription.
  3. Shaky, flashy, gimmicky, low quality ads
  4. Misleading or deceptive claims, including "get rich quick" offers
  5. Use of content or language that could offend or shock a user
  6. Landing pages that could be seen as misleading (ie it looks like an alert or notification)

Aspects that will lead to rejection:

  1. Threatening, warning, misleading ad copy, colours, imagery (incl. red frames)
  2. Unclear,warning, misleading call to action like "Start", "Fix", "Clean", "Upgrade", "Protect your phone now!", "Boost", "Scan Now", etc
  3. Any style of call to action that looks like a button â€“ ie the "download arrow" image
  4. Red notifications or alerts, low battery imagery
  5. Use of the Android robot imagery
  6. URLs that generate pop-ups or pop-unders, URLs that lead non-functioning sites or error pages, or URLs that include excessive redirects
  7. False statements, false urgency, false celebrity endorsement

Google AdX

The approval process for campaigns running on Google's AdX Exchange is a little different than for our other exchanges - even though you submit your campaigns through us, AdX reviews each campaign for compliance purposes themselves. If your campaign is rejected, we are unable to provide much information about why it was not approved.

Note: Google periodically does random checks of previously approved creatives. When this happens, you may notice that a previously approved creative that you were running becomes rejected.

Specific Google AdX Restrictions

  • Creative animations cannot exceed 30 seconds. Infinitely looping creatives will not be approved by AdX.
  • All creatives with a white or black background must have a distinct visible border.
  • Direct from banner click-to-call "tel:" links are not allowed on AdX.
  • Along with all other forbidden content, all alcohol and real-money gambling campaigns will be rejected by AdX.

Content Billing Offers on Google AdX

  • Price and billing interval information must be clearly displayed. "Clearly displayed" means written in a font that contrasts legibly with the background it is written on and is large enough to be read.
  • If the price varies depending on the user's carrier, the highest price should be the one quoted.
  • Opt-in mechanism, such as a checkbox, should be placed next to the price and billing interval.
  • If users do not proactively opt in, they should not be able to proceed down the purchase funnel. A checkbox, for example, shouldn't come pre-checked as default.
  • If the site promotes an SMS code that users text in to sign up for a subscription service, the price, billing interval, and identification as a subscription service must be clearly displayed right next to the SMS code. No opt-in mechanism is necessary.

Smaato

No Suggestive Content, Limited Gambling & Alcohol Offers

Smaato is one of the most conservative exchanges. Slightly sexy imagery is often rejected here.

Gambling and alcohol offers are ONLY permitted on Smaato if they comply with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations of the country and jurisdiction in which these ads are served.

Policy Penalties:

In order to maintain a healthy marketplace and ensure a reasonable turnaround time for campaign approvals, advertisers may have their accounts suspended and/or terminated for the following reasons:

  1. Account suspensions:
    1. Repeated uploads of creatives/landing pages that have previously been rejected by our compliance team. If we have rejected a campaign before, please do not resubmit the same creatives and/or page.
  2. Immediate Account Termination:
    1. "Bait and switching" landing pages: The landing page design that is originally used for campaign approval must never change.
    2. Malware / Spyware / Virus: The landing page initiates directly or indirectly the installation of any malware, spyware, Trojan, or other virus to the user's device.
